# Center Policies

## Document Purpose
This knowledge base document describes the operating policies of BrightPath English Learning Center, a fictional but realistic K-12 English learning center. It is written for parents, teachers, administrators, and AI systems that need accurate policy context for parent support conversations.

The policies below are intended to support the AI Parent Assistant described in the product requirements. They provide grounded information for questions about attendance, homework, assessment, communication, safety, fees, privacy, and responsible AI use. The AI should use this document to explain center rules in parent-friendly language while respecting role-based access control and educational safety rules.

## General Principles
BrightPath English Learning Center believes that children learn English best when the center, parents, and students share clear expectations. Policies are designed to protect learning time, reduce confusion, support consistent teacher feedback, and help families make informed decisions. The center aims to be firm on safety, privacy, academic honesty, and fairness while remaining flexible when families face genuine difficulties.

Parents should treat these policies as the default operating rules. When an exceptional situation occurs, staff will review the facts, document the decision, and communicate next steps through official channels. The AI Parent Assistant can explain policy language, summarize relevant guidance, and suggest who to contact, but it cannot approve exceptions, change billing outcomes, override teacher review, or access private information outside the parent's authorized student record.

## Enrollment, Placement, and Class Assignment

### Purpose
This policy explains how students are enrolled, placed into appropriate classes, and moved between levels when their learning needs change.

### Policy Details
- All new students complete a placement process before joining a regular class. The process may include a short speaking interview, a reading task, a writing sample, and a review of previous learning records.
- Placement decisions are based on English ability, age, maturity, class availability, and the student's ability to participate confidently with peers.
- Parents may request a placement review after four weeks if the class appears too difficult or too easy, but changes are not made solely because of a single low score or one difficult lesson.
- The center may recommend a bridging class when a student has uneven skills, such as strong vocabulary but weak writing control.
- Students may not join an exam preparation course without meeting the prerequisite skills listed in the course description.

### Parent Responsibilities
- Provide accurate information about the student's school grade, previous English courses, learning goals, and any recent test results.
- Avoid coaching the student to overperform during placement, because an inaccurate placement can cause stress and slow progress.
- Review the placement summary and ask questions within seven days if anything is unclear.
- Support the first month of adjustment by encouraging attendance, homework completion, and regular sleep before class.

### How Staff Apply This Policy
Teachers and administrators apply this policy consistently, but they also consider the student's age, learning history, attendance pattern, and any documented family circumstances. When a decision affects a student's class placement, assessment record, or ability to continue in a course, the center records the reason in the student profile so future conversations remain clear and fair.

### Examples
A family that knows a student will miss two classes because of a school examination period should notify the center before the absence. The teacher can then identify the lesson objectives that will be missed, the admin team can check whether a make-up class is available, and the parent can help the student complete the most important home practice before returning.

If a student repeatedly arrives late, the teacher first checks whether there is a practical barrier such as transportation or school dismissal time. If the issue continues, the center may recommend a different class schedule, a short parent meeting, or additional home routines to protect the student's learning continuity.

## Attendance and Punctuality

### Purpose
Regular attendance is essential because language learning depends on repeated exposure, guided practice, feedback, and classroom interaction.

### Policy Details
- Students are expected to attend every scheduled class unless illness, school obligations, family emergencies, or approved travel prevent attendance.
- A student is considered late if they arrive more than ten minutes after the scheduled start time.
- Attendance is recorded for every lesson and may be shown in the parent dashboard.
- If a student misses more than twenty percent of a course, the teacher may recommend level support, additional practice, or repeating selected modules.
- Late arrival may reduce participation scores when the student misses warm-up review, speaking preparation, or assessment instructions.

### Parent Responsibilities
- Notify the center before an absence whenever possible.
- Help the student review missed lesson notes and complete assigned catch-up practice.
- Contact the admin team if transportation or school schedule conflicts are causing repeated lateness.
- Check attendance records monthly and discuss patterns with the teacher before they become serious.

## Absence Notification and Make-Up Classes

### Purpose
This policy describes how the center handles missed lessons and make-up opportunities without disrupting class quality.

### Policy Details
- Make-up classes are offered when a suitable class, level, teacher, and seat are available.
- Make-up lessons are not guaranteed for every absence because classes follow different pacing and assessment schedules.
- A student may receive a recorded lesson summary, worksheet, or teacher-selected home practice when a live make-up class is not appropriate.
- Make-up classes must be requested within fourteen days of the missed lesson unless the absence was caused by a documented medical issue.
- Assessment make-ups must be scheduled separately and may require teacher approval.

### Parent Responsibilities
- Send absence information through the official communication channel rather than informal personal messages.
- Include the student's full name, class code, date of absence, and reason for absence.
- Understand that make-up classes are designed to recover core learning objectives, not to reproduce every classroom activity exactly.
- Ask the AI Parent Assistant for the missed lesson focus only after the absence has been recorded in the system.

## Homework and Independent Practice

### Purpose
Homework helps students consolidate classroom learning, build habits, and show teachers which skills need more support.

### Policy Details
- Homework is assigned according to course level and lesson objectives. Younger learners usually receive short review tasks, while older learners may receive writing, reading, vocabulary, or exam practice.
- Teachers may mark homework as complete, incomplete, late, or needs revision.
- Repeated missing homework is treated as a learning support issue first, not a discipline issue.
- Students should complete homework independently unless the teacher has specifically assigned parent-assisted practice.
- Using translation tools, answer keys, or AI to produce final answers may result in the task being marked for revision.

### Parent Responsibilities
- Create a predictable weekly study time rather than forcing long last-minute sessions.
- Praise effort, revision, and honest mistakes more than perfect scores.
- Tell the teacher if homework is consistently taking much longer than expected.
- Do not complete writing assignments for the student. Instead, ask guiding questions and encourage the student to revise.

## Assessment, Progress Reports, and Skill Scores

### Purpose
Assessments help teachers measure progress in listening, speaking, reading, writing, grammar, vocabulary, and learning habits.

### Policy Details

## Academic Integrity and Responsible AI Use

### Purpose
This policy protects honest learning and teaches students to use digital tools responsibly.

### Policy Details
- Students may use dictionaries, grammar references, and approved learning tools when the task allows them.
- Students may not submit AI-generated essays, translated paragraphs, or copied online answers as their own work.
- For writing assignments, teachers may ask students to explain vocabulary choices, grammar structures, or ideas to confirm authorship.
- During tests and active assessments, students may not use phones, smart devices, messaging apps, translation tools, or AI tools unless explicitly permitted.
- Suspected academic dishonesty is handled through a learning conversation first, followed by parent contact if the pattern continues.

### Parent Responsibilities
- Encourage the student to use AI for brainstorming, explanation, or revision practice only when allowed.
- Teach the student that mistakes are useful evidence for learning.
- Do not ask external tutors or family members to rewrite the student's assignments.
